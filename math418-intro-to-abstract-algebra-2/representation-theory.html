Representation of Finite Groups

Representation theory studies algebraic structures by representing their elements as linear transformations of vector spaces (matrices), and their operations in terms of matrix addition and multiplication.

Linear representation of a group

Let VVV be a vector space over the field C\mathbb CC, and let GL(V)GL(V)GL(V) be the group of isomorphisms of VVV to itself. i.e. GL(V)GL(V)GL(V) is the set of n×nn\times nn×n invertible matrices.

Let GGG be a finite group. The linear representation of GGG in VVV is a homomorphism ρ:G→GL(V)\rho: G \to GL(V)ρ:G→GL(V). In other words, ρ\rhoρ associates each element of GGG with an n×nn\times nn×n invertible matrix such that ρ(st)=ρ(s)ρ(t)∀s,t∈G\rho(st) = \rho(s)\rho(t) \forall s, t \in Gρ(st)=ρ(s)ρ(t)∀s,t∈G.

?? When ρ\rhoρ exists, we call VVV a representation space of GGG.
